About

Engineering practice, not a biography

I work at the intersection of product and platform: performance, reusable systems, and clear tradeoffs when the business asks for one. This is not a resume. It is what I do, how I think, and what I say no to.

Who I am

I am a senior frontend engineer—currently at Deel, on org chart and adjacent product work. Before that: Mercado Libre, Strike, and full-stack roots. The tenure matters less than a repeated pattern: I care about leaving systems better than I found them, and teaching the why, not just the what.

How I think about engineering

Frontend is the discipline of choice: what to take as a given, where to optimize, which abstraction actually pays for itself. I would rather be explicit with tradeoffs than pious with dogma. If I cannot explain the bet to a PM or a designer, I have not fully understood it yet.

Principles I return to

  1. 01 Payload, interaction, perception—users do not read your stack; they feel the result.

  2. 02 Coupling is a contract: you name it and manage it, or it costs you more later.

  3. 03 Product consistency is not cosmetic—shared systems (design, build, CI) are the abstraction tax that buys speed.

What I specialize in

Web performance, frontend platform work, and product architecture—lighthouse in the field, not only locally; bundles and loading; how UX shows up in traces; reusable foundations; language that lands with product and data, not just engineering.

What I am exploring now

Where large-app frameworks start to creak, how to measure interaction under load without hiding behind acronyms, and when AI in the dev loop is leverage versus a black box. If you are reading this, I would trade notes.
